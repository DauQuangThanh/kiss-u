# Methodology adaptations

How to shift planning language and emphasis by methodology.

## Agile / Scrum

- Frame milestones as **sprint goals** (2-week blocks).
- Highlight **velocity** (features per sprint) and **burndown**
  (work remaining).
- Emphasise the **MVP** (Minimum Viable Product) and incremental
  product releases.
- Risk planning: sprints can be adjusted; scope flexibility is a
  built-in mitigation.
- Change-requests: can enter the backlog or a sprint if approved
  by the PO.
- Communication: daily standup, sprint review, retrospective.

## Kanban

- No fixed milestones. Focus on **flow** and **cycle time**.
- Identify **WIP limits** (work in progress caps per stage).
- Risk planning: continuous delivery means rapid response to issues.
- Communication: periodic (daily or weekly) status updates;
  continuous flow metrics.
- Change-requests: can jump to the front of the queue if approved
  by stakeholders.

## Waterfall

- Milestones are **phase gates** (Requirements → Design → Dev →
  Test → Deploy). See `waterfall-phase-gates.md`.
- Each gate requires formal sign-off before moving to the next.
- Risk planning: up-front and comprehensive; change is costly.
- Communication: formal status reports at each gate; formal change
  control board.
- Change-requests: must be approved by CCB with full impact
  analysis.

## Hybrid

- Identify which parts are **fixed** (waterfall phases) and which
  are **flexible** (agile sprints).
- Risk planning: formal for fixed parts, adaptive for flexible.
- Communication: formal for fixed, agile for flexible.
- Change-requests: fixed-phase changes need CCB; backlog items
  don't.
