# Risk pre-mortem prompts

When the user can't think of risks, walk them through a pre-mortem.
The core question is always the same; the variations below surface
risks in specific categories.

## The core prompt

> Imagine it's the day after go-live and the project has failed.
> What's the most likely reason?

Write down what the user says. Then ask "what's the second most
likely reason?" and so on until they stall. Each answer becomes a
risk entry.

## Category-specific prompts

**Technical** — "What integration or infrastructure piece are you
least sure about?"

**Schedule** — "Which task on the critical path scares you most?
What would slip it by a week?"

**Resource** — "If the single most knowledgeable person on this
project won a lottery tomorrow, what would stall?"

**Budget** — "Which line item is most likely to come in double
what you estimated?"

**Scope** — "Which stakeholder is most likely to request changes
mid-project, and why?"

**External** — "What regulatory, vendor, or market change would
force a re-plan?"

## Closing the session

After surfacing risks, walk back through each one and ask:

- Likelihood 1–5? Impact 1–5?
- What's already in place to reduce this?
- Who should own monitoring this?

Record as risk-register entries using the template.
